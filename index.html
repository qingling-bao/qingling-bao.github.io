About Me

I am Qingling Bao (包青岭), an Associate Professor in the College of Statistics and Data Science, Xinjiang University of Finance & Economics (XJUFE), Urumqi, Xinjiang, China. My research focuses on eco-hydrological numerical modeling in arid inland basins, remote-sensing driven analyses of the water–food–energy–ecosystem nexus, and causal discovery/inference for high-dimensional data (e.g., transfer entropy).

I received my Ph.D. in Cartography and Geographic Information Systems from Xinjiang University (2024) under the supervision of Prof. Jianli Ding, Director of the Key Laboratory of Oasis Ecology, Ministry of Education, following my M.Sc. in Geography (2019) and B.Eng. in Surveying Engineering (2016).

My recent work includes VIC-based hydrological simulations, multi-source precipitation fusion and uncertainty analysis, CMIP6-SSP scenario-driven water consumption projections, and transfer-entropy-based hydrological process analysis.
